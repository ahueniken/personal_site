Motivation

October 15th, 2014

About a month ago my brother asked me how I stay motivated to do extra-curriculars and projects outside of school and I gave him a quick answer about signing up for things which affect other people, but I wasn't entirely satisfied with my answer. I decided that sharing my thoughts on it could hopefully be beneficial for someone.

Me and Extra-Curriculars

I've always had a very tight history with extra-curriculars. When I was extremely young my parents signed me up for activities and this naturally transitioned to me doing a little of everything in high school.

I participated in school plays, student council, sport teams and general interest clubs. Whenever an opportunity presented itself I just said "sure". Motivation never seemed to play much of a role as they were all fun.

Transitioning to University Life

However once I started university a few things changed. First and foremost being that my life is a lot less structured and you don't have the same routines and organization which provided me with convenient opportunities for projects and extra-curriculars.

I actually had a considerable amount of school work to keep up with (yay EngSci), which both drains energy and makes me wary of committing to other projects.

Projects and extra-curriculars oftentimes involve more effort and time now. As usually you're working on bigger and more exciting things and need to do more than just show-up for an hour or two.

Overcoming These Concerns & Staying Motivated

Make your project affect other people

Have deadlines

Don't battle

Start with one thing
